Frequently asked Questions

What is a coding bootcamp?
A coding bootcamp is a intensive training program that teaches students the skills they need to become proficient in software development. Our bootcamp is designed to give students a solid foundation in web development, including languages such as HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, as well as frameworks like React and NodeJS.
How long is the bootcamp for?
Our bootcamp is a full-time program that runs for 16 weeks.
Who is the bootcamp for?
Our bootcamp is suitable for anyone who is interested in learning to code, regardless of their prior experience. We have had students from a variety of backgrounds, including those who have never written a line of code before, as well as those who have some programming experience and want to take their skills to the next level.
What is the schedule like?
The bootcamp is both a full-time and part-time program. Full time students should expect to spend 40 hours per week in class and on coursework whereas part-time students can complete the program at their own pace (recommend 20 hours a week). Class hours are typically from 9:00 am to 5:00 pm, with breaks throughout the day.
What kind of support is provided during the program?
We understand that learning to code can be challenging, so we provide a variety of support resources to help our students succeed. This includes one-on-one time with instructors, weekly progress check-ins, and a dedicated student success team. We also have a strong network of alumni and industry partners who are available to offer guidance and mentorship.
What kind of job prospects can I expect?
Graduates of our bootcamp have gone on to secure jobs as web developers, software engineers, founders, freelancers, and other roles in the tech industry. We provide career support to all of our students, including resume and portfolio building, interview preparation, and job search assistance. Our career team is dedicated to helping our students find the right job opportunities and succeed in their careers.
